Local Resources

This section contains information on local agencies where you can meet with someone and get help in person with your job search. It doesn’t matter what type of work you’d like to do, each agency can help you with your job search, and help you decide what type of work might be a good match for you.

Bobby Dodd Institute

Bobby Dodd Institute (BDI) helps people with disabilities and disadvantages find jobs. BDI offers career assessments to help you find a career path that you will enjoy. BDI also provides job training and job search assistance. They help you prepare resumes and practice for interviews. They provide support and guidance during each step of the employment process.

NOBIS Works

Nobis Works helps people with disabilities find work that they will enjoy. Nobis Works offers job readiness training, vocational training, on-the-job training, and vocational assessments.

Georgia Vocational Rehabilitation Agency (GVRA)

Georgia Vocational Rehabilitation Agency (GVRA) helps people with disabilities find and keep jobs. They offer job search assistance, vocational training, and vocational assessments.

Community Connects

Community Connects offers vocational training, careers assessments, school-to-work programs, job readiness, GED preparations, job search, and job placement

Project SEARCH

Project SEARCH is a year-long school-to-work program for young people with disabilities. The program prepares you to work in the healthcare industry. Project SEARCH offers classroom instruction, career exploration, internships, and job placement.

Goodwill of Georgia

Goodwill helps people with disabilities, limited education, or limited job skills find jobs. Goodwill offers job search resources, helps you create a resume and cover letter, provides access to employment opportunities, and provides job skill training.

Briggs & Associates

Briggs & Associates helps people find and maintain jobs. They offer a career development program to help you find a job. The also offer continued support after you get a job.

Disability Service Center

Disability Service Center (DSC) offers services to help people with disabilities live independent, meaningful lives. DSC offers workshops and seminars to prepare you for employment and independent living.

Who can use their services?


You can use BDI’s services if you have a physical and/or developmental disability.
You can also use their services if you have a disability due to chronic illness such as diabetes, arthritis, or mental illness.

What services do they offer?


BDI provides evaluations and assessments to help you identify your strengths and your barriers to employment. You can explore your interests, strengths, and aptitudes. You can also review your previous work experience. These evaluations help you set career goals and find a path to employment.

BDI also provides career counseling case management. Career counselors support and guide you during job training and throughout the job search.

Specialized Job Training


BDI offers three Career Academies. Career Academies are specialized job training programs for people with disabilities. These programs focus on teaching the skills that you need to be successful in a specific job. The Career Academies offer hands-on training programs and classroom based certifications.

BDI offers three Career Academies


1. Logistics and Warehousing : This Career Academy provides specialized job training in logistics and warehousing. You can obtain a Certified Logistics Technician certification. You can also get hands-on job training at partner companies.

2. Office Essentials : This Career Academy helps you learn skills for an office environment. You learn customer service skills and computer skills. You learn how to use the internet, Microsoft Windows, and Microsoft Word, Excel, Access, Outlook, and PowerPoint. This Career Academy is a five-week program.

3. Food Service : This career Academy provides hands-on training in the food service industry. You have the opportunity to work at McKenney’s Café. McKenney’s Café is a sandwich shop on the campus of McKenney’s Inc.

In the future, BDI will offer more Career Academies. They will have CareerAcademies that prepare you for careers in customer service, call center operations, janitorial services, and landscaping. Job Connections Services

BDI Programs


The Job Readiness and Job Connections Program prepares you to apply for a job. This program helps you create a resume and fill out a job application. You can also participate in mock interviews and learn important job-search skills. Employment specialists help you find job opportunities.

The Job Coaching Program is a program for newly-hired employees. When you get a job, a job coach will work with you during your first weeks on the job to help you adjust to your new work environment.

BDI also offers internships and on-the-job training. Internships can help you and your prospective employer work together before you are hired full-time. BDI pays you during the internship.

What services do they offer?


Vocational Assessment

The vocational assessment program helps you figure out what you are interested in and what you are good at. This program offers the following assessments:
- The TABE (Tests Adult Basic Education)
- Personal Assessments- Learning Style, Personality, Work Values
- Transition Inventories- To Work, College Survival, Job Survival
- Mail Sorting, Physical Exertion, Color Coding and Production
- The WRAT (Wide Range Achievement Test)
- The RAI (Reading & Arithmetic)
- The EAS (Employee Aptitude Survey)
- SDS (Career Decision Making)

Work Evaluation

In the work evaluation program, you perform actual work on a variety of tasks. You are evaluated on you work stamina, vocational interests and aptitude, your ability to get along with others, and your motivation to work.
After you are evaluated, you meet with a counselor and a program manager. These people help you choose other programs that will help you become a better worker.

Employment Services

In the employment services program, you do real work at the Nobis Works’ Tommy Nobis Center, or at a company like the WellStar warehouse, Kroger, or Owens & Minor. You learn proper workplace behavior and social skills. You receive support and on-the-job training from a job coach.
The employment services program also provides the following services:
- Classroom instruction
- GED preparation
- Job readiness
- Work practices
- Mobility training (use of public transit)

Supported Employment

In the supported employment program, Nobis Works provides training and support to help you find and keep a job at a community business. Nobis Works provides the following services:

- Job coaching
- Job sampling
- Assessment
- Job readiness
- Job development
- Intensive job site training
- Counseling
- Referral information
- Family support
- Development of natural supports in the workplace

Skills Training

Nobis Works offers three different classes to prepare you to work in an office environment. Each class lasts eight weeks.

The classes are:
1. Introduction to Computers
2. Beginning Office Technology (BOT)
3. Customer Service Representative/Call Center Training

What services do they offer?

Orientation

When you begin the program, you will spend your first few weeks working on the following things:

* New employee orientation
* Hands-on skill assessment
* Familiarization with the business environment
* Career plan development

Employment Skills Training

You will work on the following employment skills:

* Team building
* Getting around the workplace
* Workplace safety
* Technology
* Social skills
* Communication
* Presentation skills
* Interviewing skills
* Money management
* Health and wellness
* Job search skills
* Keep a job skills

Internships/Worksite Rotations

You will complete three internships. These internships are similar to clinical rotations because they are unpaid. You will work with your
teachers, job coaches, and business liaisons to find worksites that fit your skills.

Job Placement

After you have completed three internships, your vocational rehabilitation counselor helps to place you in a job that fits your
experiences, strengths, and skills.

Career Portfolio

After you complete the program, you will receive a career portfolio containing the following resources:

* A resume
* Letters of recommendation
* A competency profile
* Any awards or special recognitions received during the program

Career Centers

Workforce Development Program

The Workforce Development Program helps you prepare for a job. Services include:

- Evaluation of work-related skills
- Evaluation of work experience
- Training at Goodwill stores

- Vocational evaluations
- Job readiness training
- Job placement

Vocational Evaluation

The vocational evaluation helps you identify your hopes, goals, and interests. This evaluation helps you find a career path.

Work Evaluation

Goodwill offers a work evaluation program to assess your job skills. You are evaluated on attendance, punctuality, work quality and
quantity, supervisor relations, co-worker relations, stamina, conformation to rules, communication, decision-making skills, and initiative.

Community-Based Work Adjustment Training

This program helps you adjust to a new job. Services include:

- Orientation to workplace expectations
- Maintaining positive attitudes and behaviors
- Building a successful resume and practicing for interviews
- Providing work experience and opportunities to explore employment options
- Information about job retention and career advancement
